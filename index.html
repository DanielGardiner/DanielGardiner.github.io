ABOUT ME

I am a recent graduate of the Mayden Academy Full Stack Training Course. During the course I worked on a mix of greenfield projects and legacy code, working within an agile team where I actively contributed towards sprint planning, reviews and retrospectives. During these projects I used industry standard methodologies such as OOP, SOLID principles, design patterns, frameworks, unit testing, git and task automation managers.

Prior to completing the Mayden Academy Training Course I worked as a Senior Infectious Disease Epidemiologist for Public Health England, where I worked for 7 years. This involved investigating outbreaks of disease, conducting research studies, and working extensively with R/RStudio to manipulate and analyse data.

I have also worked as a Consultant for the World Health Organization where, over the course of a year, I automated the production of influenza surveillance reports for over 50 European Regions using R/RStudio.

Career Change Motivation

Whilst working as an epidemiologist I would actively seek out as many opportunities as possible involving writing code, problem solving and building useful tools. However, I had many non-tech related responsibilities which would draw me away from the thing I enjoyed doing most (writing code!). I therefore decided to take the plunge and change career in order to make the things I'm passionate about the focus of my daily activities.
